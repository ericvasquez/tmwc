Advantages of Mesquite wood and charcoal

Mesquite wood and charcoal burns very slowly, longer, and will generate a lot of heat for a longer period of time, so you can use less Mesquite wood or charcoal when you barbeque. The smoke that it generates gives a very distinct flavor to meats that you barbeque, and that Texans love. Across Texas, it is most often used to prepare smoked barbecue, from steak, chicken, pork, to venison and other exotic meats. Buy a bag of Texas Mesquite Wood & Charcoal for your next backyard barbeque.

Igniting the Charcoal.

To ignite the Mesquite charcoal or wood, soak a paper towel in cooking oil, place some charcoal or wood on the soaked paper towel, and light with a lighter. Using cooking oil, means that your bargeque will not have that petroleum taste to your meats as it cooks, like some charcoal briquettes do. As a result, Texas Mesquite Charcoal will provide a more consistent natural burn without excessive smoking ensuring that the natural flavor of food is enhanced and not masked.
